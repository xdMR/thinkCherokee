Meet Your Guide

JW Webster

“With the right teacher, you will learn how to think like a Cherokee so you can speak like a Cherokee.”

I was raised in a traditional Cherokee home. For me, being traditional is a way of life I was raised with my language and the Cherokee language as with all other languages offer its speakers alternative ideas and other possible views of how one sees the world. I grew up with family members who were traditional healers who understood the importance of our teachings and how it allows us to see that we are a part of this world and not the masters of it. They taught me that being humble, thankful and joyful no matter how little we had was empowering. I have always had tremendous respect for our Elders who retained and nurtured the true Cherokee way of life and I am so thankful that I listened to their stories and teachings.... From the age of 14, I was spending my time visiting Elders and recording old terms and words that were no longer being used in order to save that knowledge. It was just twenty years later that I myself started to find it difficult to speak with someone in Cherokee due to the lack of speakers. I have spent my entire life believing in a single goal--that I have to find a way to make learning Cherokee more understandable and easier for you! Studying old forms of written Cherokee and comparing our ancestors ability to use the language with today's form of speech made it possible. I noticed that traditional people still spoke the old way, even today and that the difference in speech was caused by loss of Cherokee thought and belief in the old practices. It was then very clear to me that our traditional way of life was far more endangered than our language. Therefore, I developed a teaching method that would allow students to acquire Cherokee thought. By explaining traditional practices and customs, and how they shape the beliefs behind the meaning of the words, my students were able to see the world from a Cherokee perspective which gave them valuable insight with regards to how we speak Cherokee.
